I’m a young dedicated student, studying at the University of Reading, aspiring to become a Computer Scientist. I grew up in a small country, well the smallest in-land country in Africa, called The Gambia. It has a mere 80km of coastline, but what a magnificent stretch it encompasses: golden beaches backed by swaying palms and sprinkled with scenic lagoons, sleepy fishing villages and biologically rich coastal reserves. It should be on your bucket list as one of the must visit countries. I arrived in the UK in 2016 to do my A-levels in Manchester. The past year, I started university, and at the time of writing this, completed my first year. I came to study at Codenation to find a meaningful activity to do during my summer break. It has been a delight to study here, learning about new people,their different and unique backgrounds. Furthermore, the tutors here have been most helpful and friendly, and I am honoured to have learned a lot from. To conclude this poor documentation about myself, I would like to leave a message to any reader out there. My homeland moniker is ‘the smiling coast of Africa’. So smile, like Mother Teresa said “ Let us always meet each other with a smile, for the smile is the beginning of love.”
